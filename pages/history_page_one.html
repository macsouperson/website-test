Early (pre-)Islamic History

Islamic history, as taught by most, often starts with the pre-Islamic era, establishing the culture, societal, economy, and history of Arabia to provide some context to the region where Islam emerged.

While this has some utility, it can perhaps be overwhelming for a novice to the subject. So rather than languishing on the pre-Islamic period, it will suffice to say that the Arabian peninsula and surrounding Levant were home to diverse groups of people. It is critical to note that the Roman and Sassanid empires surrounding Arabia and there was some degree of contact between these regional powers and the denizens of Arabia.
