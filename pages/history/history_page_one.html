The Beginnings

pre-Islamic History

Islamic history, as taught by most, often starts with the pre-Islamic era, establishing the culture, societal, economy, and history of Arabia to provide some context to the region where Islam emerged. While this has some utility, it can perhaps be overwhelming for a novice to the subject.

Additionally, from the theological perspective, Islam does not begin in 7th century, but rather co-incides with the God's creation of the universe and its inhabitants.

Indeed your Lord is Allah Who created the heavens and the earth in six Days,then established Himself on the Throne. He makes the day and night overlap in rapid succession. He created the sun, the moon, and the stars—all subjected by His command. The creation and the command belong to Him alone. (Quran, 7:54)

(pre-)Islamic Arabia

So rather than languishing on the pre-Islamic period, it will suffice to say that the Arabian peninsula and surrounding Levant were home to diverse groups of people.The Arabian peninsula and surrounding area were religiously diverse. The the west were Christian Byzantines, and to the east were the Zoroastrian Sassanians. Within the region, Christian, Jews, pagans, and monotheistic hanifs. Urban centers such as Mecca were centers of commerce and were surrounded by nomadic Bedouin tribes, sharing in a ___ relationship. Mecca served as both a commercial and spiritual hub. The ka'ba, a holy site for the surrounding tribes, housing dieties from several of the pagan religions afforded Mecca and its tribes certain distinctions.
