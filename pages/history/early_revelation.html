Early Revelation

Revelation

Revelation first occuring to the Prophet around the 40th year of this life1. He began to experience visions in this sleep and would retreat to the cave in mount Hira, where we would pray and separate himself from the social ills he witnessed in Mecca. It had become a practice, that for one month out of the year the Prophet spent alone in the cave at Hira. During one of his sojourns to the cave, he experienced an overwhelming force, commanding him to read. This overwhelming pressure, which squeezed the breath from his lungs was the angel Gabriel, repeatedly extolled him, commanding him to read in the name of the Lord who created him.

The first verses of the were:

Recite in the Name of thy Lord Who created created man from a blood clot. Recite! Thy Lord is most noble, Who taught by the Pen, taught man that which he knew not. (al-Alaq, 1-5)

After this experience, the Prophet descended down the mount, returning to Khadija, filled with fear and in shock2. Khadija comforted the Prophet, certain that God would not punish him. She took the Prophet to her cousin, a learned man Waraqa bin Nawfal, who was familiar with Scripture, being either a hanif or a Christian. Waraqa confirmed Prophet Muhammed's status as a messenger of God even expressing contrition at not being able to support the Prophet during the inevitable tribulations he would face.

Following the revelations of the first five verses of surah al-Alaq, the next revelation came to the Prophet, though there are differing narratives as to which verses followed the initial revelation. Some assert, the next revelation was:

Nūn. By the pen and that which they inscribe, thou art not, by the blessing of thy Lord, possessed. Truly thine shall be a reward unceasing. And truly thou art of an exalted character. So thou wilt see and they will see (al-Qalam, 1-5)
While other state that it was the first two verses of surah al-Muddaththir, "O thou who art covered, arise and warn!" (al-Muddaththir, 1-2). These latter verses referring to the Prophet being wrapped up by Khadija upon returning from Hira following the first revelation. The former verses from al-Qalam also point to the Prophet's anxieties upon receiving revelation, as he may have worried he was possessed, rather than divinely inspired.

Soon after, Waraqa passed away and Prophet recieved no further revelation for some time, with some narrations saying that this period lasted around two years... Following this period, al-Duha and al-Sharh revealed to the Prophet. However, the exact chronology of revelation is not a settled matter. After the initial revelation and subsequent intermission, revelation began to occur more frequently.

Notes

  1. Some narrations state that he was 43 at the time of revelation
  2. In Tabari, ibn Kathir, and Bukhari there is mention that the Prophet became distraught, though the inciting events differ. Tabari states that after receiving revelation at Hira, the Prophet considered flinging himself from the mountain. However, in Bukhari, this occurs after the death of Waraqa ibn Nawfal and the temporary cessation of revelation. By contrast, in Sahih Muslim, these events are entirely omitted.
